# Source Policy

The report should be useful, current, and honest about uncertainty. Prefer a smaller number of high-quality sources over a broad pile of low-quality summaries.

## Source Hierarchy

Use primary sources for factual claims:

- SEC filings and other regulatory filings.
- Earnings releases and shareholder letters.
- Earnings call transcripts.
- Investor presentations.
- Company press releases.
- Official regulatory or legal documents.

Use secondary sources for interpretation:

- Reputable financial news.
- Analyst or market commentary available to the user.
- Public investor letters.
- Short-seller reports or adversarial reports, when relevant.
- Sector commentary and peer results.

Avoid relying on:

- Low-quality SEO summaries.
- Unsourced social posts.
- Aggregated snippets with no original source.
- Stale articles that predate the current narrative shift.

## Source Pack

Build this table before writing the report:

| Source | Type | Date | Why It Matters | Claims Supported |
| ------ | ---- | ---- | -------------- | ---------------- |
| [Title/link] | [Type] | [Date] | [Why this source matters] | [Claim ids or short claim text] |

Use these source types:

- Official company source
- Filing
- Transcript
- Financial news
- Market commentary
- Investor letter
- Short-seller / adversarial
- Regulatory / legal
- Social / retail narrative
- Other

## Claim Extraction

Extract claims before writing prose:

| Claim | Source | Date | Type | Side | Confidence | Related Metric |
| ----- | ------ | ---- | ---- | ---- | ---------- | -------------- |
| [Claim] | [Source] | [Date] | [Type] | [Side] | [High/Medium/Low] | [Metric] |

- demand
- revenue growth
- margin
- earnings
- valuation
- competitive position
- product

- credibility
- accounting
- customer concentration
- supplier dependency

Sides:

- bull

- neutral
- consensus
- counter-narrative
- adversarial

## Source Rules

- Prefer primary sources for facts.
- Use commentary sources for interpretation, framing, and sentiment.
- Label adversarial sources clearly.
- Treat allegations as allegations unless established by primary evidence.
- Cite major claims.
- Identify source gaps in the final report.
- If source coverage is weak, say: "Source coverage is insufficient for a high-confidence report. Here is a partial report and the specific sources or data needed to improve it."
